# Follow-up

Return visits for established patients. The clinical approach varies based on treatment stage and goals of the visit.

## A. Test Results + Non-Surgical Treatment

For patients returning to:
- Review investigation results
- Discuss diagnostic confirmation
- Continue or modify conservative treatment
- Reassess symptoms and progress

**Key Goals**:
- Explain test findings in plain language
- Confirm or refine diagnosis
- Review treatment response
- Plan next steps with If-Then contingencies

---

## B. Post-Op Acute (Complication Monitoring)

Early postoperative period (typically 0-6 weeks) focusing on:
- Wound healing assessment
- Complication surveillance (infection, DVT, nerve injury)
- Pain management
- Initial mobilization guidance

**Key Goals**:
- Identify complications early
- Ensure safe wound healing
- Confirm adherence to weight-bearing restrictions
- Plan progression to next phase

---

## C. Post-Op Subacute (Functional Recovery)

Later postoperative period (typically 6 weeks+) focusing on:
- Functional recovery and rehabilitation
- Return to activities
- Long-term outcome assessment
- Discharge planning

**Key Goals**:
- Monitor functional progress
- Guide rehabilitation advancement
- Manage patient expectations
- Plan return to work/sport
- Determine discharge or ongoing follow-up needs
