# Follow-up: Post-Op Acute - Short Presentation

## a. History Taking

**Format**: "[TIME] post [PROCEDURE]. Pain [status], wound [status], adhering to [restrictions]."

**Example**: "2 weeks post right ankle arthroscopy and lateral ligament reconstruction. Pain well-controlled, wound dry, adhering to NWB in boot."

**Key Elements**:
- Time post-op
- Procedure
- Pain control
- Wound status
- Restriction adherence

---

## b. Examination

**Format**: "Examination: wound [status], [swelling level], neurovascularly intact."

**Example**: "Examination: wound clean and dry, sutures intact, moderate swelling, DP pulse palpable, sensation intact, toes warm and pink."

**Key Elements**:
- Wound appearance
- Swelling assessment
- Neurovascular status
- Any concerns

---

## c. Impression

**Format**: "[TIME] post [PROCEDURE], progressing [assessment]."

**Example**: "2 weeks post right ankle arthroscopy and lateral ligament reconstruction, progressing satisfactorily with no complications."

**Key Elements**:
- Procedure recap
- Overall progress assessment
- Complications: present or absent

---

## d. Recommendation

**Format**: "Plan: [wound care], continue [restrictions] for [duration], [next milestone], review [timeframe]."

**Example**: "Plan: sutures removed today, continue NWB in boot for 4 more weeks, commence active ROM exercises, X-ray and review at 6 weeks to assess progression to WBAT."

**Key Elements**:
- Wound management action
- Current restrictions and duration
- Next phase/milestone
- Follow-up timing and purpose

---

## Complete Presentation Template

"[AGE] [SEX], [TIME] post [PROCEDURE performed DATE]. [Pain status], [wound status], [adherence]. Examination: [wound], [swelling], neurovascularly intact. Impression: progressing [assessment], no complications. Plan: [wound action], continue [restrictions] until [TIME], [next phase], review [TIMEFRAME] for [PURPOSE]."

**Example**:

"52M, 3 weeks post right TAR performed 15/12/25. Pain controlled with paracetamol, anterior wound healing well, adhering to NWB. Examination: anterior wound healed, sutures removed, mild residual swelling, foot warm, DP palpable, sensation intact. X-rays: satisfactory prosthesis position, no subsidence. Impression: 3 weeks post TAR, satisfactory early post-operative course, no complications. Plan: continue NWB in boot for 3 more weeks, active ROM exercises commenced, review at 6 weeks with repeat X-ray to assess progression to WBAT in boot."
