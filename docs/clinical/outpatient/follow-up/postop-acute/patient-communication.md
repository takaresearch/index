# Follow-up: Post-Op Acute - Patient Communication

## a. History Taking

### Opening
- How are you feeling after the surgery?
- How has the recovery been so far?

### Pain Assessment
- How is the pain? Is it manageable?
- Are the pain medications helping?
- Is the pain getting better, staying the same, or getting worse?

### Wound Check
- How does the wound look to you?
- Any redness, swelling, or drainage?
- Has the dressing stayed clean and dry?

### Adherence to Restrictions
- Have you been able to follow the weight-bearing restrictions?
- Are you using the crutches / boot / support as instructed?
- Any difficulties with the restrictions?

### Red Flags
- Any fever or chills?
- Any increasing redness or warmth around the wound?
- Any numbness or tingling that's new or getting worse?
- Any calf pain or swelling?

---

## b. Examination

### Preparation
- Let me take a look at how things are healing.
- I'll check the wound and make sure everything looks good.

### Wound Assessment
- The wound is healing well / looks good / shows [finding].
- I can see the sutures/staples are intact.
- There's [no / minimal / moderate] swelling, which is [normal / to be expected / concerning].

### Neurovascular Check
- Your circulation looks good.
- Sensation is intact.
- You can move your toes well.

---

## c. Impression

### Healing Status
- Overall, you're progressing appropriately / well / as expected for [TIME] after surgery.
- The wound is healing nicely / satisfactorily / with no concerns.

### Complication Screening
- There are no signs of infection / DVT / nerve injury / other complications.
- Everything looks as it should at this stage.

### Address Concerns
- The [pain / swelling / limitation] you're experiencing is normal at this stage.
- OR: I'm a bit concerned about [finding], so we'll [action].

---
