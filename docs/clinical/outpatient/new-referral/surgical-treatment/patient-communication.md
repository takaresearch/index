# New Referral: Surgical Treatment - Patient Communication

## a. History Taking

### Opening
- Good morning/afternoon. Thank you for coming in. I understand you've been having problems with your [LOCATION].

### Confirm Prior Treatment
- I see you've tried [physiotherapy / orthotics / injections]. How did that go?
- How long did you give it? Did it help at all?
- What made you decide to seek surgical consultation?

### Current Symptoms
- How are things now? What's your biggest limitation?
- What would you most like to be able to do that you can't do now?

### Patient Goals & Expectations
- What's most important to you right now? Work, sports, daily activities?
- What are your goals for treatment?
- Have you had surgery before? How do you feel about the possibility of surgery?

---

## b. Examination

### Preparation
- Let me examine your [LOCATION] to confirm the findings and make sure surgery is the right option for you.

### Focused Assessment
- (Perform systematic examination)
- I can confirm [KEY FINDINGS that support surgical indication].

---

## c. Impression

### Diagnosis Confirmation
- Based on your history, examination, and imaging, you have [DIAGNOSIS].
- This is causing [FUNCTIONAL IMPACT].

### Why Surgery
- Given that conservative treatment hasn't provided enough relief, I believe surgery would be the best option for you.
- Surgery can help by [SPECIFIC BENEFIT: stabilizing, correcting deformity, removing damaged tissue].

---

## d. Recommendation

### Surgical Plan
- I recommend [SPECIFIC PROCEDURE].
- This involves [SIMPLE DESCRIPTION OF WHAT WILL BE DONE].

### Benefits
- The main benefits are: [improved stability / pain relief / functional improvement / deformity correction].
- This should allow you to [RETURN TO ACTIVITIES / REDUCE PAIN / IMPROVE QUALITY OF LIFE].

### Risks
- As with any surgery, there are risks. The main ones include:
  - Infection (though this is uncommon)
  - Nerve injury, which could cause numbness or tingling
  - Stiffness or reduced range of motion
  - Blood clots (DVT/PE)
  - The problem could come back (recurrence)
  - Anaesthetic risks

### Recovery Timeline
- After surgery, you'll need to [NON-WEIGHT-BEARING / WEIGHT-BEARING IN BOOT] for about [DURATION].
- You'll be in a [CAST / BOOT / SPLINT] for [DURATION].
- Physiotherapy will start at [TIMEFRAME] and focus on [GOALS].
- Full recovery typically takes [TIMEFRAME], though this varies from person to person.
- You'll need to take [DURATION] off work, depending on your job.

### What if I Don't Have Surgery?
- If we don't treat this, [PROBLEM] may get worse over time, potentially leading to [LONG-TERM CONSEQUENCES].
