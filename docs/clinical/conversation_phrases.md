# 会話フレーズ集（患者との対話用）

このページは、診察室で患者と直接コミュニケーションを取る際に使用する実践的な英語フレーズ集です。挨拶から診断説明、治療計画の共有、手術の説明まで、外来診療の各段階で使用できるフレーズを整理しています。

## 1. 挨拶・自己紹介

### 初診時

- Good morning / Good afternoon. My name is Dr. Taka Ogawa. I'm from Japan, and I'll be taking care of you today.
- Hello, [PATIENT]. Thank you for coming in. I'm Dr. Taka Ogawa, and I'm a foot and ankle surgeon. I'm originally from Japan.
- Nice to meet you. I'm Dr. Taka Ogawa. Please, have a seat.

### 再診時

- Good to see you again, [PATIENT]. How have you been?
- Welcome back, [PATIENT]. How are things going since we last met?
- It's good to see you again. Let's see how you're doing.

### 名前の発音について（必要に応じて）

- My name is Taka Ogawa—you can call me Dr. Ogawa if that's easier.

---

## 2. 主訴の確認

### オープンクエスチョン

- What brings you in today?
- What's the main problem you're experiencing?
- Tell me about what's been bothering you.
- What would you like me to help you with today?

### 機能障害としての主訴の確認

- What is the main thing that's bothering you? Is it the pain itself, or is there something you're not able to do because of it?
- What activities have become difficult for you?
- What's the biggest limitation in your daily life right now?

---

## 3. 問診フレーズ

### 症状の性質

- Can you describe the pain for me? Is it sharp, dull, aching, or burning?
- Where exactly do you feel the pain? Can you point to it?
- Does the pain stay in one place, or does it spread or radiate?
- How would you rate the pain on a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 is no pain and 10 is the worst pain you can imagine?

### 時間経過

- When did this start?
- Was there a specific injury or event that triggered it?
- Has it been getting better, worse, or staying about the same?
- Does it come and go, or is it constant?

### 機能への影響

- How far can you walk before it becomes a problem?
- Are stairs difficult for you?
- Can you stand for long periods?
- Does it affect your sleep?
- Have you had to change your activities at work or home?

### 増悪・軽快因子

- What makes it worse? Walking, standing, rest, or certain movements?
- What makes it better? Rest, medication, or specific positions?
- Is it worse in the morning, during the day, or at night?

### 腫脹・炎症徴候

- Have you noticed any swelling?
- Does the swelling get worse during the day?
- Is the area warm or red?
- Does it feel stiff, especially in the morning?

### 神経症状

- Do you have any numbness or tingling?
- Any weakness or feeling of instability?
- Does it feel like pins and needles?

### 既往治療

- Have you tried any treatments for this?
- What have you done so far? Physiotherapy, medication, injections, or braces?
- How long did you try [TREATMENT]? Did it help at all?
- Are you currently taking any medications for this?

---

## 4. 身体所見の説明（患者への説明）

### 診察の開始

- Now, let me take a look at your [FOOT/ANKLE/LEG]. I'm going to examine the area, check the movement, and see where the pain is.
- Please let me know if anything hurts or if you feel uncomfortable.
- I'm going to touch different areas—tell me if any spot is particularly tender.

### 診察中の説明

- I can see some swelling here.
- There's tenderness when I press here.
- The range of motion is a bit limited.
- The alignment looks [normal / slightly off].
- I notice [DEFORMITY / SWELLING / SKIN CHANGES].

### 診察の終了

- Thank you. That's helpful. Let me explain what I found.

## 5. 診断の説明

### 診断の提示

- Based on what you've told me and what I found on examination, I believe you have [DIAGNOSIS].
- The symptoms and examination findings are consistent with [DIAGNOSIS].
- Essentially, this means [SIMPLE EXPLANATION IN PLAIN LANGUAGE].

### 診断の説明（平易な言葉で）

**Plantar fasciitis**:
- This is inflammation of the thick band of tissue on the bottom of your foot. It's a common cause of heel pain.

**Achilles tendinopathy**:
- This is a problem with the tendon at the back of your heel. It can become painful and swollen, often due to overuse.

**Ankle instability**:
- The ligaments that hold your ankle stable have been stretched or damaged, which can cause the ankle to feel unstable or give way.

**Hallux valgus (bunion)**:
- This is a deformity where the big toe angles toward the other toes, creating a bony bump on the side of the foot.

**Osteoarthritis**:
- This is wear and tear of the joint cartilage, which can cause pain, stiffness, and reduced movement.

### 鑑別診断の説明（必要に応じて）

- There are a few possibilities we need to consider. The most likely is [DIAGNOSIS], but we should also rule out [ALTERNATIVE].
- To be sure, I'd like to order some tests to confirm the diagnosis.

## 7. 治療計画の説明

### 保存療法の提案

- I recommend we start with non-surgical treatment. This includes [TREATMENT OPTIONS].
- Let's try [PHYSIOTHERAPY / ORTHOTICS / MEDICATION / INJECTION] for [DURATION] and see how you respond.
- Most people improve with these conservative measures.

### 治療の目的と期待値

- The goal is to [REDUCE PAIN / IMPROVE FUNCTION / PREVENT PROGRESSION].
- This should help with [SPECIFIC SYMPTOM].
- It may take [TIMEFRAME] to see improvement, so please be patient.

### 治療の期限と評価

- We'll try this for [DURATION], and then we'll reassess.
- If you're not better by [TIMEFRAME], we'll need to consider other options.

## 8. 手術の説明（Informed Consent）

### 手術の提案

- Given that conservative treatment hasn't provided enough relief, I believe surgery would be the best option for you.
- Surgery can help by [SPECIFIC BENEFIT: e.g., stabilizing the ankle, correcting the deformity, removing damaged tissue].

### 手術のリスクとベネフィット

**Benefits**:
- The main benefits are [IMPROVED STABILITY / PAIN RELIEF / FUNCTIONAL IMPROVEMENT / DEFORMITY CORRECTION].
- This should allow you to [RETURN TO ACTIVITIES / REDUCE PAIN / IMPROVE QUALITY OF LIFE].

**Risks**:
- As with any surgery, there are risks. The main ones include:
  - Infection (though this is uncommon)
  - Nerve injury, which could cause numbness or tingling
  - Stiffness or reduced range of motion
  - Blood clots (DVT/PE)
  - The problem could come back (recurrence)
  - Anaesthetic risks

**Natural history (手術をしない場合)**:
- If we don't treat this, [PROBLEM] may get worse over time, potentially leading to [LONG-TERM CONSEQUENCES: e.g., more pain, further joint damage, increased difficulty with activities].

### 術後回復の説明

- After surgery, you'll need to [NON-WEIGHT-BEARING / WEIGHT-BEARING IN BOOT] for about [DURATION].
- You'll be in a [CAST / BOOT / SPLINT] for [DURATION].
- Physiotherapy will start at [TIMEFRAME] and focus on [GOALS].
- Full recovery typically takes [TIMEFRAME], though this varies from person to person.
- You'll need to take [DURATION] off work, depending on your job.

### 意思決定の確認

- I've explained the procedure, the risks, and the expected recovery. Do you have any questions?
- I'd like you to take some time to think about this. There's no rush—we can discuss it again at your next visit.
- If you decide to proceed, we can schedule the surgery. Just let us know when you're ready.

  A: It depends on your job. If you have a desk job, you should be fine. If your work involves [STANDING / WALKING / HEAVY LIFTING], you may need to modify your activities or take some time off.

- **Q: Do I really need surgery?**

  A: You can take [PARACETAMOL / NSAIDs] as needed. If the pain is severe, let me know and we can discuss stronger options.

## 15. 実践的なヒント

### コミュニケーションの原則

- **Listen actively**: Give the patient time to speak, and acknowledge their concerns.
- **Use plain language**: Avoid medical jargon. If you must use technical terms, explain them.
- **Check for understanding**: Ask "Does that make sense?" or "Do you have any questions?"
- **Be empathetic**: Acknowledge the patient's pain and concerns.
- **Set realistic expectations**: Be honest about what treatment can and cannot achieve.

### 時間管理

- If time is limited, prioritize: (1) chief complaint, (2) key examination findings, (3) diagnosis and plan, (4) follow-up.
- For complex cases, consider scheduling a longer appointment or a follow-up to discuss options in detail.

### 文化的背景の考慮

- As a surgeon from Japan, you may encounter cultural differences. Be respectful and open to different perspectives on health and treatment.
- Some patients may prefer more direct communication, while others may prefer a more gradual approach. Adjust your style as needed.

- Are you with me so far?
- Do you follow what I'm saying?

### 同意の確認

## 注意事項

- このフレーズ集は一般的なガイドラインです。個々の患者の状況、文化的背景、言語能力に応じて調整してください。
- 患者の反応を観察し、理解度や感情的な状態に合わせてコミュニケーションスタイルを変えることが重要です。
- 専門用語を使う場合は、必ず平易な言葉で説明を追加してください。
- 時間が限られている場合は、最も重要な情報（診断、治療計画、フォローアップ）に焦点を当ててください。
