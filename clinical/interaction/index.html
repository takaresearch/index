    問診・インターラクションの型（患者との対話）¶

    このページは、患者とのインターラクションを「漏れなく・偏りなく・過剰に侵襲的でなく」進めるための問診の型を定義します。重要なのは、情報収集そのものよりも、(1)患者が語る困りごとを正確に定義し、(2)臨床仮説の検証に必要十分な追加質問へ段階的に移り、(3)意思決定の前提（価値観・生活背景・許容リスク）を同定することです。

    1. 最初の一文（主訴の同定）¶

    目的¶

    主訴を「痛い」ではなく、機能障害として定義します。主訴が曖昧なまま鑑別に入ると、診断は偶然当たってもプランがぶれます。

    型¶

    • いちばん困っていることは何ですか（痛み以外も含む）
    • それが起きるのは、どの動作／どの場面ですか（歩行距離、階段、片脚立位、靴、仕事）
    • そのために、何ができなくなりましたか（具体的に）

    2. 受傷／発症の基点と時間軸¶

    • いつからですか（起点）
    • きっかけはありますか（外傷／過負荷／靴変更／運動量変化）
    • どのように変化しましたか（急性→改善／慢性進行／波がある）

    3. 仮説駆動の追加質問（過不足を減らす）¶

    目的¶

    鑑別を列挙するのではなく、「どの仮説を否定／支持したいか」に沿って質問します。

    典型軸¶

    • 荷重との関係：荷重痛／安静時痛／夜間痛
    • 局在：一点か、広がるか（放散）
    • 腫脹：夕方増悪、圧痕、熱感
    • 神経症状：しびれ、灼熱感、誘発肢位
    • 炎症性徴候：朝のこわばり、対称性、他関節

    4. 患者の意思決定前提（価値観と制約）¶

    治療の選択は、解剖学的な正しさだけでなく、患者の価値観と制約の上で成立します。ここを曖昧にすると、説明が「正しいが受け入れられない」形になりやすくなります。

    • 生活上の優先順位（仕事・育児・介護・スポーツ）
    • 受け入れ可能なダウンタイム（免荷期間、通院頻度）
    • 望まない結果（瘢痕、可動域制限、疼痛遷延）
    • 手術への態度（恐怖、過去の経験、情報源）

    5. 説明の型（期待値調整）¶

    目的¶

    患者説明は「情報提供」ではなく、治療目標と観察ポイントを共有し、次回分岐（AならB）を患者に理解可能な形で提示することです。

    型¶

    • 現時点の見立て：
    • いま一番大事な点：
    • 今日やること：
    • 次回こうだったら（A）→ こうします（B）
    • 逆にこうなら（C）→ こうします（D）

    6. 公開運用上の注意¶

    この型は一般論であり、個別症例の記載は行いません。症例ベースのメモが必要な場合は、公開外（private/フォルダなど）に分離し、匿名化・規程遵守を前提とします。

    7. 実践スクリプト（挨拶から診察終了まで）¶

    以下は、外来での実際の問診の流れを、医師の立場からの会話例として示したものです。患者の反応に応じて柔軟に調整してください。

    Phase 1: Opening and chief complaint¶

    Physician: Good morning/afternoon, [PATIENT]. Thank you for coming in today. My name is Dr. [NAME], and I'll be taking care of you. Please, have a seat.

    Physician: What brings you in today?

    (Listen to patient's response)

    Physician: I see, you're having pain in your [LOCATION]. Let me ask you a bit more about this. What is the main thing that's bothering you? Is it the pain itself, or is there something you're not able to do because of it?

    Phase 2: Functional impact¶

    Physician: When does the pain bother you the most? For example, when you're walking, going up or down stairs, first thing in the morning—which activities are most difficult?

    Physician: How far can you walk before it becomes a problem? Has that changed compared to before?

    Physician: Are there things in your daily life or at work that you've had to stop doing or do differently because of this?

    Phase 3: Onset and timeline¶

    Physician: When did this start?

    Physician: Was there anything that might have triggered it? For example, did you twist your ankle, start a new exercise, change your shoes, or increase your activity level?

    Physician: Since it started, has it been getting better, worse, or does it come and go?

    Phase 4: Hypothesis-driven questions¶

    Physician: Does the pain happen mainly when you're standing or walking, or does it hurt even when you're resting?

    Physician: Does it ever wake you up at night?

    Physician: Have you noticed any swelling or warmth? Does it change during the day?

    Physician: Do you have any numbness or tingling sensations?

    Phase 5: Prior treatments¶

    Physician: Have you tried any treatments for this?

    (Listen to patient's response)

    Physician: How long did you try [TREATMENT]? Did it help at all?

    Phase 6: Patient values and constraints¶

    Physician: What's most important to you right now in your daily life? Is there something you really need to be able to do—for work, family, sports, or hobbies?

    Physician: If treatment is needed, how much time off or modified activity would be manageable for you? Are there times that would be particularly difficult, like around work commitments or family responsibilities?

    Physician: How do you feel about the possibility of surgery, if it were to come to that? Have you had surgery before?

    Phase 7: Physical examination transition¶

    Physician: Okay, let me take a look now. I'm going to examine your [LOCATION], check the movement and see where the pain is. Please let me know if anything hurts, okay?

    (Perform physical examination)

    Phase 8: Assessment and plan¶

    Physician: Thank you. Let me explain what I think is going on.

    Physician: Based on what you've told me and what I've found on examination, I believe you have [DIAGNOSIS]. Essentially, [BRIEF EXPLANATION].

    Physician: Today, I'd like to order [INVESTIGATION] to confirm the diagnosis. We should have the results in about [TIMEFRAME].

    Physician: In the meantime, I recommend [CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT: e.g., anti-inflammatory medication, a brace, physiotherapy]. This should help with [PURPOSE].

    Phase 9: Follow-up and contingency planning¶

    Physician: I'd like to see you back in [TIMEFRAME] to review the results and decide on the next steps together.

    Physician: If the test shows [FINDING A], then we'll need to consider [TREATMENT B: e.g., an injection or possibly surgery].

    Physician: On the other hand, if [FINDING C], then we can continue with the current treatment and monitor how you're doing.

    Physician: Between now and then, if things get suddenly worse, or if you notice [RED FLAG: e.g., increasing numbness, spreading swelling], please don't wait for your appointment—call us right away.

    Phase 10: Questions and closing¶

    Physician: Do you have any questions? Please feel free to ask about anything, no matter how small.

    (Address patient's questions)

    Physician: Alright, I'll see you in [TIMEFRAME]. Take care, and I hope you feel better soon.

    Notes on using this script¶

    • This script is a general framework. Adjust the order, skip sections, or add detail based on the patient's responses and the available time.
    • Creating a comfortable atmosphere is most important. Avoid a rapid-fire interrogation; listen actively and acknowledge the patient's concerns.
    • Use plain language and avoid jargon. Check for understanding.
    • If time is limited, prioritize Phases 1-3 and 7-10, and select from Phases 4-6 as needed.
