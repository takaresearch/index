    会話フレーズ集（患者との対話用）¶

    このページは、診察室で患者と直接コミュニケーションを取る際に使用する実践的な英語フレーズ集です。挨拶から診断説明、治療計画の共有、手術の説明まで、外来診療の各段階で使用できるフレーズを整理しています。

    1. 挨拶・自己紹介¶

    初診時¶

    • Good morning / Good afternoon. My name is Dr. Taka Ogawa. I'm from Japan, and I'll be taking care of you today. (professional, warm)
    • Nice to meet you. I'm Dr. Taka Ogawa. Please, have a seat. (friendly, casual)
    • Hello, [PATIENT]. Thank you for coming in. I'm Dr. Taka Ogawa, and I'm a foot and ankle surgeon. I'm originally from Japan. (professional, informative)
    • Good morning, [PATIENT]. It's a pleasure to meet you. I'm Dr. Taka Ogawa, and I'll be conducting your examination today. (formal, polite)

    再診時¶

    • Good to see you again, [PATIENT]. How have you been? (friendly)
    • Welcome back, [PATIENT]. How are things going since we last met? (warm, professional)
    • It's good to see you again. Let's see how you're doing. (casual, friendly)
    • Hello again, [PATIENT]. I trust you've been well since our last visit. (formal, polite)

    名前の発音について（必要に応じて）¶

    • My name is Taka Ogawa—you can call me Dr. Ogawa if that's easier. (casual, friendly)
    • Please feel free to call me Dr. Ogawa if that's more comfortable for you. (polite)

    2. 主訴の確認¶

    オープンクエスチョン¶

    • What brings you in today? (casual, direct)
    • What's the main problem you're experiencing? (friendly, professional)
    • Tell me about what's been bothering you. (warm, conversational)
    • What would you like me to help you with today? (friendly)
    • How may I assist you today? (formal, polite)
    • Could you please describe what concerns have brought you to see me? (formal, polite)

    機能障害としての主訴の確認¶

    • What is the main thing that's bothering you? Is it the pain itself, or is there something you're not able to do because of it? (friendly, thorough)
    • What activities have become difficult for you? (direct, professional)
    • What's the biggest limitation in your daily life right now? (casual, empathetic)
    • I'd like to understand how this is affecting your daily activities. Could you describe what has become challenging for you? (formal, empathetic)

    3. 問診フレーズ¶

    症状の性質¶

    • Can you describe the pain for me? Is it sharp, dull, aching, or burning? (friendly, direct)
    • Where exactly do you feel the pain? Can you point to it? (casual)
    • Does the pain stay in one place, or does it spread or radiate? (professional)
    • How would you rate the pain on a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 is no pain and 10 is the worst pain you can imagine? (standard clinical)
    • Could you please describe the nature of the pain you're experiencing? (formal, polite)
    • I'd appreciate if you could indicate the precise location of your discomfort. (formal, polite)

    時間経過¶

    • When did this start? (casual, direct)
    • Was there a specific injury or event that triggered it? (friendly)
    • Has it been getting better, worse, or staying about the same? (conversational)
    • Does it come and go, or is it constant? (direct)
    • Could you tell me when you first noticed these symptoms? (polite)
    • Has there been any change in the severity since onset? (formal, clinical)

    機能への影響¶

    • How far can you walk before it becomes a problem? (direct, practical)
    • Are stairs difficult for you? (casual)
    • Can you stand for long periods? (direct)
    • Does it affect your sleep? (friendly, concerned)
    • Have you had to change your activities at work or home? (conversational)
    • Could you describe how this condition is impacting your daily activities? (formal, polite)
    • I'd like to understand the extent to which this affects your mobility. (formal, professional)

    増悪・軽快因子¶

    • What makes it worse? Walking, standing, rest, or certain movements? (direct, helpful)
    • What makes it better? Rest, medication, or specific positions? (friendly)
    • Is it worse in the morning, during the day, or at night? (clinical)
    • Are there any activities or positions that exacerbate the discomfort? (formal, clinical)
    • Have you identified any factors that provide relief? (polite, professional)

    腫脹・炎症徴候¶

    • Have you noticed any swelling? (casual, direct)
    • Does the swelling get worse during the day? (conversational)
    • Is the area warm or red? (direct, clinical)
    • Does it feel stiff, especially in the morning? (friendly)
    • Have you observed any inflammation or changes in appearance? (formal, clinical)

    神経症状¶

    • Do you have any numbness or tingling? (direct)
    • Any weakness or feeling of instability? (casual, quick)
    • Does it feel like pins and needles? (conversational, descriptive)
    • Have you experienced any sensory changes, such as numbness or paresthesia? (formal, clinical)

    既往治療¶

    • Have you tried any treatments for this? (casual)
    • What have you done so far? Physiotherapy, medication, injections, or braces? (friendly, comprehensive)
    • How long did you try [TREATMENT]? Did it help at all? (conversational)
    • Are you currently taking any medications for this? (professional)
    • Could you describe any previous treatments you've undertaken? (formal, polite)
    • What has been your experience with prior interventions? (formal, clinical)

    4. 身体所見の説明（患者への説明）¶

    診察の開始¶

    • Now, let me take a look at your [FOOT/ANKLE/LEG]. I'm going to examine the area, check the movement, and see where the pain is. (friendly, informative)
    • Please let me know if anything hurts or if you feel uncomfortable. (caring, casual)
    • I'm going to touch different areas—tell me if any spot is particularly tender. (direct, helpful)
    • I'd like to conduct a physical examination now. Please inform me if you experience any discomfort. (formal, polite)
    • With your permission, I'll now examine the affected area. (very polite, formal)

    診察中の説明¶

    • I can see some swelling here. (neutral, observational)
    • There's tenderness when I press here. (clinical, matter-of-fact)
    • The range of motion is a bit limited. (professional)
    • The alignment looks [normal / slightly off]. (casual, clinical)
    • I notice [DEFORMITY / SWELLING / SKIN CHANGES]. (neutral)
    • I'm observing some [FINDING] in this area. (formal, clinical)

    診察の終了¶

    • Thank you. That's helpful. Let me explain what I found. (friendly, appreciative)
    • Thank you for your cooperation. I'll now discuss my findings with you. (formal, polite)

    5. 診断の説明¶

    診断の提示¶

    • Based on what you've told me and what I found on examination, I believe you have [DIAGNOSIS]. (friendly, professional)
    • The symptoms and examination findings are consistent with [DIAGNOSIS]. (clinical, straightforward)
    • Essentially, this means [SIMPLE EXPLANATION IN PLAIN LANGUAGE]. (casual, educational)
    • After reviewing your history and examination, my assessment is that you have [DIAGNOSIS]. (formal, professional)
    • The clinical presentation suggests [DIAGNOSIS]. (formal, clinical)

    診断の説明（平易な言葉で）¶

    Plantar fasciitis: - This is inflammation of the thick band of tissue on the bottom of your foot. It's a common cause of heel pain. (casual, clear) - You have inflammation in the plantar fascia, which is the supportive structure on the sole of your foot. (formal, descriptive)

    Achilles tendinopathy: - This is a problem with the tendon at the back of your heel. It can become painful and swollen, often due to overuse. (friendly, explanatory) - The Achilles tendon has developed a degenerative condition, typically related to repetitive strain. (formal, clinical)

    Ankle instability: - The ligaments that hold your ankle stable have been stretched or damaged, which can cause the ankle to feel unstable or give way. (conversational, descriptive) - There is laxity in the lateral ankle ligaments, resulting in mechanical instability. (formal, clinical)

    Hallux valgus (bunion): - This is a deformity where the big toe angles toward the other toes, creating a bony bump on the side of the foot. (casual, clear) - This condition involves angular deviation of the first metatarsophalangeal joint. (very formal, clinical)

    Osteoarthritis: - This is wear and tear of the joint cartilage, which can cause pain, stiffness, and reduced movement. (friendly, understandable) - This represents degenerative changes to the articular cartilage, a progressive condition. (formal, clinical)

    鑑別診断の説明（必要に応じて）¶

    • There are a few possibilities we need to consider. The most likely is [DIAGNOSIS], but we should also rule out [ALTERNATIVE]. (friendly, thorough)
    • To be sure, I'd like to order some tests to confirm the diagnosis. (casual, practical)
    • We must consider several differential diagnoses. I recommend further investigation to establish a definitive diagnosis. (formal, clinical)

    6. 検査・画像の説明¶

    検査の提案¶

    • I'd like to order [X-RAY / MRI / CT / ULTRASOUND] to get a better picture of what's going on. (friendly, conversational)
    • This will help us confirm the diagnosis and plan the best treatment. (practical, clear)
    • The results should be available in about [TIMEFRAME]. (informative)
    • I recommend obtaining [IMAGING] to better characterize the condition. (formal, professional)
    • It would be prudent to perform [INVESTIGATION] for a comprehensive assessment. (formal, clinical)

    画像所見の説明¶

    • The X-ray shows [FINDING IN PLAIN LANGUAGE]. (casual, clear)
    • The MRI confirms [DIAGNOSIS] and shows [RELEVANT DETAILS]. (professional, informative)
    • The good news is [POSITIVE FINDING]. However, we can see [CONCERNING FINDING]. (friendly, balanced)
    • The imaging demonstrates [FINDING]. (formal, clinical)
    • The radiographic findings reveal [FINDING], which is consistent with our clinical assessment. (formal, professional)

    7. 治療計画の説明¶

    保存療法の提案¶

    • I recommend we start with non-surgical treatment. This includes [TREATMENT OPTIONS]. (professional, clear)
    • Let's try [PHYSIOTHERAPY / ORTHOTICS / MEDICATION / INJECTION] for [DURATION] and see how you respond. (friendly, collaborative)
    • Most people improve with these conservative measures. (reassuring, casual)
    • I propose we initiate conservative management with [TREATMENT]. (formal, professional)
    • The recommended approach is to commence non-operative treatment. (formal, clinical)

    治療の目的と期待値¶

    • The goal is to [REDUCE PAIN / IMPROVE FUNCTION / PREVENT PROGRESSION]. (direct, clear)
    • This should help with [SPECIFIC SYMPTOM]. (casual, practical)
    • It may take [TIMEFRAME] to see improvement, so please be patient. (friendly, realistic)
    • Our objective is to [TREATMENT GOAL] while minimizing intervention. (formal, professional)
    • The anticipated outcome is [EXPECTED RESULT] over the course of treatment. (formal, clinical)

    治療の期限と評価¶

    • We'll try this for [DURATION], and then we'll reassess. (casual, practical)
    • If you're not better by [TIMEFRAME], we'll need to consider other options. (conversational, planning)
    • We shall implement this treatment plan and reevaluate at [TIMEFRAME]. (formal, professional)

    8. 手術の説明（Informed Consent）¶

    手術の提案¶

    • Given that conservative treatment hasn't provided enough relief, I believe surgery would be the best option for you. (professional, direct)
    • Surgery can help by [SPECIFIC BENEFIT: e.g., stabilizing the ankle, correcting the deformity, removing damaged tissue]. (friendly, informative)
    • Considering the limited response to conservative measures, I recommend we discuss surgical intervention. (formal, professional)
    • In light of the persistent symptoms, a surgical approach would be most appropriate. (formal, clinical)

    手術のリスクとベネフィット¶

    Benefits: - The main benefits are [IMPROVED STABILITY / PAIN RELIEF / FUNCTIONAL IMPROVEMENT / DEFORMITY CORRECTION]. - This should allow you to [RETURN TO ACTIVITIES / REDUCE PAIN / IMPROVE QUALITY OF LIFE].

    Risks: - As with any surgery, there are risks. The main ones include: - Infection (though this is uncommon) - Nerve injury, which could cause numbness or tingling - Stiffness or reduced range of motion - Blood clots (DVT/PE) - The problem could come back (recurrence) - Anaesthetic risks

    Natural history (手術をしない場合): - If we don't treat this, [PROBLEM] may get worse over time, potentially leading to [LONG-TERM CONSEQUENCES: e.g., more pain, further joint damage, increased difficulty with activities].

    術後回復の説明¶

    • After surgery, you'll need to [NON-WEIGHT-BEARING / WEIGHT-BEARING IN BOOT] for about [DURATION].
    • You'll be in a [CAST / BOOT / SPLINT] for [DURATION].
    • Physiotherapy will start at [TIMEFRAME] and focus on [GOALS].
    • Full recovery typically takes [TIMEFRAME], though this varies from person to person.
    • You'll need to take [DURATION] off work, depending on your job.

    意思決定の確認¶

    • I've explained the procedure, the risks, and the expected recovery. Do you have any questions? (professional, thorough)
    • I'd like you to take some time to think about this. There's no rush—we can discuss it again at your next visit. (friendly, supportive)
    • If you decide to proceed, we can schedule the surgery. Just let us know when you're ready. (casual, collaborative)
    • Please take the time you need to consider this information. We can arrange a follow-up discussion at your convenience. (formal, polite)
    • I encourage you to reflect on this decision. Should you wish to proceed, please inform us, and we shall make the necessary arrangements. (very formal, polite)

    9. フォローアップの説明¶

    次回予定¶

    • I'd like to see you back in [TIMEFRAME] to review how you're doing. (friendly, clear)
    • We'll review the test results and decide on the next steps together. (collaborative, professional)
    • Please make an appointment for [DATE / TIMEFRAME]. (direct, practical)
    • I would like to schedule a follow-up appointment in [TIMEFRAME]. (formal, professional)
    • Please arrange a review consultation for [TIMEFRAME] at your convenience. (formal, polite)

    分岐の説明（If-Then）¶

    • If the symptoms improve with the current treatment, we'll continue and gradually reduce the support. (casual, encouraging)
    • If the symptoms persist or get worse, we'll need to consider [NEXT STEP: e.g., injection, surgery, further imaging]. (professional, contingency)
    • If the test shows [FINDING A], then we'll proceed with [TREATMENT B]. (clear, logical)
    • On the other hand, if [FINDING C], we can continue with conservative management. (conversational)
    • Should the conservative approach prove unsuccessful, we shall reassess and consider alternative interventions. (formal, professional)

    緊急時の対応¶

    • If things get suddenly worse, or if you notice [RED FLAGS: e.g., increasing numbness, spreading redness, severe pain], please don't wait for your appointment—call us right away or go to the emergency department. (urgent, caring)
    • If you have any concerns before your next visit, please contact the clinic. (friendly, accessible)
    • Should you experience any concerning symptoms, please seek immediate medical attention. (formal, serious)

    10. 術後フォローアップ¶

    術後経過の確認¶

    • How are you feeling after the surgery? (friendly, caring)
    • How is the pain? Is it manageable? (direct, concerned)
    • Are you able to follow the weight-bearing restrictions? (professional, checking)
    • Any concerns or problems? (casual, open)
    • How has your postoperative course been? (formal, professional)
    • Are you experiencing any difficulties with the prescribed protocol? (formal, clinical)

    術後所見の説明¶

    • The wound is healing well. (reassuring, clear)
    • The swelling is normal at this stage—it should gradually improve. (friendly, informative)
    • The X-rays look good. The [IMPLANT / FUSION / REPAIR] is in the right position. (casual, positive)
    • The surgical site is healing appropriately. (formal, clinical)
    • The radiographic findings demonstrate satisfactory positioning. (formal, technical)

    術後計画の説明¶

    • You can now start [WEIGHT-BEARING / REMOVE BOOT / INCREASE ACTIVITY]. (direct, encouraging)
    • Continue with the exercises we discussed. (casual, reminding)
    • We'll see you again in [TIMEFRAME] to check your progress. (friendly, scheduled)
    • You are now cleared to progress to [NEXT PHASE]. (formal, authorizing)
    • Please continue with your rehabilitation program as outlined. (formal, instructive)

    11. よくある質問への対応¶

    治療期間について¶

    • Q: How long will this take to get better?
      A: Most people start to see improvement within [TIMEFRAME], but full recovery can take [TIMEFRAME]. It varies from person to person.

    運動・活動について¶

    • Q: Can I still exercise / play sports?
      A: For now, I'd recommend avoiding [HIGH-IMPACT ACTIVITIES]. You can continue with [LOW-IMPACT ACTIVITIES: e.g., swimming, cycling] if they don't cause pain.

    仕事への影響¶

    • Q: Can I still work?
      A: It depends on your job. If you have a desk job, you should be fine. If your work involves [STANDING / WALKING / HEAVY LIFTING], you may need to modify your activities or take some time off.

    手術の必要性¶

    • Q: Do I really need surgery?
      A: Surgery is one option, but it's not always necessary. Let's try conservative treatment first. If that doesn't work after [TIMEFRAME], then we can discuss surgery again.

    痛みの管理¶

    • Q: What can I take for the pain?
      A: You can take [PARACETAMOL / NSAIDs] as needed. If the pain is severe, let me know and we can discuss stronger options.

    12. 終わりの挨拶¶

    質問の確認¶

    • Do you have any questions? Please feel free to ask about anything, no matter how small. (friendly, open)
    • Is there anything else you'd like to know? (casual, inviting)
    • Does that make sense? Let me know if you'd like me to explain anything again. (conversational, supportive)
    • Please don't hesitate to ask if you have any questions or concerns. (formal, polite)
    • Are there any aspects you would like me to clarify further? (formal, professional)

    閉めの言葉¶

    • Alright, I'll see you in [TIMEFRAME]. Take care, and I hope you feel better soon. (friendly, warm)
    • Thank you for coming in today. We'll be in touch about [NEXT STEP]. (professional, clear)
    • Feel free to call if you have any concerns before your next visit. (casual, accessible)
    • Have a good day, and take care of yourself. (warm, caring)
    • Thank you for your time today. I look forward to seeing you at your next appointment. (formal, polite)
    • I wish you a smooth recovery. Please contact us should any issues arise. (formal, professional)

    13. 文化的配慮（必要に応じて）¶

    言語の確認¶

    • Do you prefer to speak in English, or would you like an interpreter? (considerate, practical)
    • If anything I said wasn't clear, please let me know—I want to make sure you understand everything. (friendly, caring)
    • Would you be more comfortable with an interpreter present? (polite, accommodating)
    • Please inform me if you require interpretation services for our discussion. (formal, professional)

    理解度の確認¶

    • Let me make sure I've explained this clearly. Can you tell me what you understand about [DIAGNOSIS / TREATMENT PLAN]? (friendly, checking)
    • I want to make sure we're on the same page. What questions do you have? (casual, collaborative)
    • May I ask you to summarize your understanding to ensure clarity? (formal, educational)
    • I would like to confirm your comprehension of the treatment plan. (formal, clinical)

    14. 困難な状況への対応¶

    患者が不安な場合¶

    • I understand this can be worrying. Let's take it one step at a time. (empathetic, reassuring)
    • It's normal to feel anxious about [SURGERY / DIAGNOSIS]. We'll work through this together. (supportive, collaborative)
    • I appreciate this is a concerning situation. Please know that I'm here to support you through this process. (formal, empathetic)
    • Your concerns are completely understandable. Allow me to address them comprehensively. (formal, validating)

    患者が治療に消極的な場合¶

    • I understand that [SURGERY / TREATMENT] can seem daunting. Let's discuss your concerns. (friendly, open)
    • What are your main worries? Let's address them one by one. (casual, systematic)
    • I respect your hesitation. May we discuss what aspects concern you most? (formal, respectful)
    • I would like to understand your reservations so we can address them appropriately. (formal, professional)

    予後が不確実な場合¶

    • It's difficult to predict exactly how this will progress, but based on similar cases, [TYPICAL OUTCOME]. (honest, informative)
    • We'll monitor you closely and adjust the treatment as needed. (reassuring, flexible)
    • While the exact trajectory is uncertain, we will provide close monitoring and modify our approach as indicated. (formal, clinical)

    15. 実践的なヒント¶

    コミュニケーションの原則¶

    • Listen actively: Give the patient time to speak, and acknowledge their concerns.
    • Use plain language: Avoid medical jargon. If you must use technical terms, explain them.
    • Check for understanding: Ask "Does that make sense?" or "Do you have any questions?"
    • Be empathetic: Acknowledge the patient's pain and concerns.
    • Set realistic expectations: Be honest about what treatment can and cannot achieve.

    時間管理¶

    • If time is limited, prioritize: (1) chief complaint, (2) key examination findings, (3) diagnosis and plan, (4) follow-up.
    • For complex cases, consider scheduling a longer appointment or a follow-up to discuss options in detail.

    文化的背景の考慮¶

    • As a surgeon from Japan, you may encounter cultural differences. Be respectful and open to different perspectives on health and treatment.
    • Some patients may prefer more direct communication, while others may prefer a more gradual approach. Adjust your style as needed.

    16. よく使う表現のバリエーション¶

    痛みの確認¶

    • Where does it hurt? (casual, direct)
    • Can you show me where the pain is? (friendly, practical)
    • Point to where it's most painful. (direct, simple)
    • Could you please indicate the location of your discomfort? (formal, polite)

    理解度の確認¶

    • Does that make sense? (casual, checking)
    • Are you with me so far? (friendly, conversational)
    • Do you follow what I'm saying? (casual, checking)
    • Is my explanation clear? (professional)
    • Do you have a clear understanding of what I've explained? (formal, thorough)

    同意の確認¶

    • Does that sound reasonable? (casual, collaborative)
    • Are you comfortable with this plan? (friendly, checking)
    • Does this work for you? (casual, flexible)
    • Is this approach acceptable to you? (formal, respectful)
    • Do you consent to proceed with this treatment plan? (formal, clinical)

    追加情報の依頼¶

    • Tell me more about that. (casual, inviting)
    • Can you give me more details? (friendly, curious)
    • What else should I know? (direct, open)
    • Could you elaborate on that point? (formal, professional)
    • I would appreciate additional details regarding this matter. (formal, polite)

    注意事項¶

    • このフレーズ集は一般的なガイドラインです。個々の患者の状況、文化的背景、言語能力に応じて調整してください。
    • 患者の反応を観察し、理解度や感情的な状態に合わせてコミュニケーションスタイルを変えることが重要です。
    • 専門用語を使う場合は、必ず平易な言葉で説明を追加してください。
    • 時間が限られている場合は、最も重要な情報（診断、治療計画、フォローアップ）に焦点を当ててください。

    トーンの使い分けについて¶

    各フレーズには以下のようなトーン表記が付けられています：

    • casual: カジュアルで親しみやすい。関係が構築された患者や若い患者に適切
    • friendly: 温かく親しみやすいが、プロフェッショナルな距離感を保つ。多くの状況で使いやすい
    • professional: 専門的で信頼感がある。標準的な医療コミュニケーション
    • formal: 丁寧で格式ばった表現。初診、高齢者、または正式な場面に適切
    • polite: 礼儀正しく配慮のある表現。デリケートな話題や不安な患者に適切
    • clinical: 医学的・技術的な表現。専門的な説明や記録に適切
    • empathetic: 共感的で支持的。不安や悩みを抱える患者に適切

    推奨事項: - 初診では professional または formal なトーンから始め、患者の反応を見て調整する - 関係が構築されたら、friendly や casual なトーンで親近感を高める - 深刻な診断や手術の説明では、formal と empathetic を組み合わせる - 若い患者には casual から friendly が効果的 - 高齢者や初対面の患者には formal または polite が安全
