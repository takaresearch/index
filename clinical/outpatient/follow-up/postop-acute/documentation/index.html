    Follow-up: Post-Op Acute - Documentation¶

    a. History Taking¶

    Template¶

    Postoperative Day/Week: [NUMBER] post [PROCEDURE], performed [DATE]

    Pain: - Level: [VAS score or descriptive] - Adequately controlled with [medication] - [Improving / stable / increasing trend]

    Wound: - Patient reports [dry / no drainage / concerning symptoms] - Dressing: [intact / changed / saturated]

    Mobility: - Adhering to [NWB / PWB / WBAT] restrictions - Using [crutches / walker / boot] appropriately - Managing at home / requiring assistance

    Medications: - Taking [ANALGESIA] as prescribed - DVT prophylaxis: [agent, compliance] - Other: [antibiotics if indicated]

    Complications Screened: - No fever, chills, or systemic symptoms - No signs of wound infection - No symptoms of DVT/PE - No new neurological symptoms

    b. Examination¶

    Template¶

    Wound: - Appearance: [clean, dry, intact / erythema / drainage / dehiscence] - Sutures/staples: [intact / partial removal / removed] - Swelling: [minimal / moderate / significant] - Temperature: [normal / warm / hot]

    Neurovascular: - Sensation: [intact / altered - distribution] - Motor: [intact / weakness] - Perfusion: [good capillary refill, pulses palpable / concerns]

    Range of Motion (if appropriate to assess): - [Measured ROM, note if limited by pain, swelling, or restriction]

    Post-Operative Imaging (if obtained): - X-ray ([DATE], [AP/Lateral/Oblique/Mortise views]): - Hardware: [type, location, position - satisfactory / concerns] - Screws: [number, trajectory, purchase in bone] - Plate: [position, contouring] - Pins/wires: [location, crossing fracture/osteotomy site]

    • Alignment: [maintained / anatomic / [degrees] of deformity]

      • Compare to immediate post-op: [stable / displaced]
      • Measurements: [angles, distances as relevant]

    • Bone healing: [too early / early callus / progressive healing]

      • Fracture/osteotomy gap: [maintained / closing / completely healed]
      • Arthrodesis site: [bone contact adequate / gap / bridging]

    • Complications:

      • Hardware loosening: [absent / suspected]
      • Loss of reduction: [no / yes - describe]
      • Other: [periosteal reaction / hematoma calcification]

    Imaging Interpretation: Post-operative X-rays demonstrate [hardware in satisfactory position / alignment maintained / early healing as expected]. No immediate complications visualized. [Any concerns or need for close monitoring.]

    c. Impression¶

    Template¶

    Postoperative Course: - [NUMBER days/weeks] post [PROCEDURE] - Progress: [satisfactory / appropriate / concerning]

    Wound Healing: [progressing normally / delayed / complication identified]

    Complications: [None identified / specific concern listed]

    Assessment: Patient progressing [well / appropriately / with concerns] in the acute postoperative period following [PROCEDURE]. [No complications identified / specific issue requiring attention].

    d. Recommendation¶

    Template¶

    Plan:

    1. Wound Care:
    2. [Suture removal performed / planned for TIMEFRAME]
    3. Continue [dressing protocol]

    4. Keep dry and clean

    5. Weight-Bearing:

    6. Continue [NWB / PWB / WBAT] for [DURATION]
    7. [Device - boot, crutches] until [DATE / NEXT VISIT]

    8. Progress to [NEXT PHASE] at [TIMEFRAME]

    9. Immobilization:

    10. [Boot / splint / cast] for [DURATION]

    11. Remove for: [exercises / hygiene / as specified]

    12. Rehabilitation:

    13. [Active ROM exercises / physiotherapy referral]
    14. Begin at [TIMEFRAME]

    15. Focus on [specific goals]

    16. Medications:

    17. Analgesia: [continue / wean / PRN]
    18. DVT prophylaxis: [continue for DURATION]

    19. Other: [as indicated]

    20. Imaging:

    21. X-ray at [TIMEFRAME] to assess [hardware position / alignment / healing]

    22. Follow-up:

    23. Review in [TIMEFRAME]
    24. Purpose: [wound check / X-ray review / progress assessment / weight-bearing advancement]

    Safety Net: - Return immediately or attend ED if: - Fever >38°C - Wound: increasing redness, warmth, purulent drainage, dehiscence - DVT/PE symptoms: calf pain/swelling, chest pain, shortness of breath - Neurovascular: new/worsening numbness, weakness, coldness - Severe uncontrolled pain

    Patient Education: - Expected recovery phase: acute healing (weeks 0-6) - Swelling is normal and may take months to fully resolve - Importance of adherence to restrictions to protect surgical repair
