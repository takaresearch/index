# Outpatient Phrasebook (English-only)

This page collects useful English phrases for outpatient orthopaedic notes and referral letters. The goal is to build a personal phrasebook that you can draw on repeatedly, helping you write clear, professional letters more efficiently. Placeholders like `[PATIENT]`, `[SIDE]`, `[DIAGNOSIS]`, `[DURATION]`, `[IMAGING]`, and `[FOLLOW-UP]` can be replaced with specific details. Remember to avoid including any identifying information when sharing examples.

## Quick vocabulary (for repeated writing)

### Time and course

- **acute / subacute / chronic**
- **intermittent / persistent**
- **progressive / stable**
- **atraumatic onset / following an injury**
- **recurrent / episodic**

### Symptom vocabulary

- **pain, tenderness, swelling, stiffness**
- **instability, giving way**
- **numbness, tingling, weakness**
- **catching, locking**
- **limp, reduced walking tolerance**

### Examination vocabulary

- **inspection / palpation / range of motion**
- **alignment / deformity**
- **neurovascularly intact**
- **tenderness over [STRUCTURE]**
- **pain at end range**
- **provocative test**

### Imaging vocabulary

- **plain radiographs (X-rays)**
- **MRI / CT / ultrasound**
- **joint space narrowing**
- **osteophytes**
- **subchondral sclerosis / cysts**
- **malalignment**

### Assessment language

- **consistent with**
- **most likely**
- **differential diagnosis**
- **rule out**
- **red flags**

## 1) Opening and purpose (clinic note / letter)

- It was a pleasure to see **[PATIENT]** today for assessment of **[PROBLEM]**.
- Thank you for referring **[PATIENT]** for evaluation of **[PROBLEM]**.
- **[PATIENT]** returns today for follow-up of **[DIAGNOSIS/PROBLEM]**.
- I reviewed **[PATIENT]** today regarding **[PROBLEM]**, with a focus on **[GOAL]**.

## 2) History of presenting complaint

### Core descriptors

- The pain began **[ONSET]** and has been present for **[DURATION]**.
- Symptoms are **[IMPROVING / WORSENING / UNCHANGED]** over time.
- The pain is located over the **[LOCATION]** and is **[SHARP / DULL / ACHING / BURNING]**.
- Symptoms are aggravated by **[ACTIVITY]** and relieved by **[REST / MEDICATION / OFFLOADING]**.
- **[PATIENT]** reports **[INSTABILITY / CATCHING / LOCKING / GIVING WAY]**.
- There is **[NO / INTERMITTENT / PERSISTENT]** swelling.

### Functional framing (high yield)

- The main limitation is **[FUNCTIONAL LIMITATION]**.
- **[PATIENT]** is unable to **[ACTIVITY]** because of pain.
- Walking tolerance is approximately **[DISTANCE / TIME]**.
- Stairs are **[DIFFICULT / NOT POSSIBLE]** due to **[PAIN / INSTABILITY]**.

### Prior management

- Prior management has included **[PHYSIOTHERAPY / ORTHOTICS / NSAIDs / INJECTION / IMMOBILIZATION]**.
- This provided **[GOOD / PARTIAL / MINIMAL]** relief.
- **[PATIENT]** has tried **[TREATMENT]** for **[DURATION]** without sustained benefit.

## 3) Past history and risk modifiers (brief)

- Relevant comorbidities include **[DIABETES / SMOKING / RA / OSTEOPOROSIS]**.
- There is no history of inflammatory arthropathy.
- **[PATIENT]** is **[NOT]** taking anticoagulants.
- Allergies: **[ALLERGIES]**.

## 4) Examination (phrases that read well)

- On examination, the skin is intact with no erythema or warmth.
- There is tenderness over the **[STRUCTURE]**.
- Swelling is **[MILD / MODERATE / SIGNIFICANT]**.
- Range of motion is **[FULL / LIMITED]**, with pain at **[END RANGE / THROUGHOUT]**.
- Neurovascular status is intact distally.
- The hindfoot alignment is **[NEUTRAL / VARUS / VALGUS]**.
- **[TEST]** is **[POSITIVE / NEGATIVE]**.

## 5) Imaging and investigations

- Plain radiographs demonstrate **[FINDINGS]**.
- There is evidence of **[OSTEOPHYTES / JOINT SPACE NARROWING / SUBCHONDRAL SCLEROSIS]** consistent with **[OA]**.
- MRI shows **[FINDINGS]**.
- CT is requested to assess **[BONE STOCK / ARTHRITIS EXTENT / ALIGNMENT]**.

## 6) Impression (diagnosis statement)

- Overall, this presentation is most consistent with **[DIAGNOSIS]**.
- The leading differential diagnoses include **[DDX 1]** and **[DDX 2]**.
- At present, there is no clinical evidence of **[RED FLAG]**.

## 8) Shared decision-making / consent-style phrases

- We discussed the options of **[OPTION A]** versus **[OPTION B]**, including expected recovery and potential complications.
- The potential benefits are **[BENEFITS]**; the main risks include **[RISKS]**.
- **[PATIENT]** understands the above and wishes to proceed with **[PLAN]**.
- **[PATIENT]** would like time to consider surgery; we will reassess in **[TIMEFRAME]**.

## 9) Postoperative instructions (generic phrasing)

- Postoperatively, the patient will be **[NWB / PWB / WBAT]** for **[DURATION]**.
- Immobilization in **[SPLINT / CAST / BOOT]** for **[DURATION]**.
- DVT prophylaxis: **[AGENT]** for **[DURATION]**, unless contraindicated.
- Wound check at **[TIMEFRAME]**; suture removal at **[TIMEFRAME]**.
- Physiotherapy to commence at **[TIMEFRAME]** focusing on **[GOALS]**.

## 10) Letter closing (professional tone)

- Thank you again for the referral.
- Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions.
- I will review **[PATIENT]** in **[FOLLOW-UP]** to reassess and finalize the plan.
