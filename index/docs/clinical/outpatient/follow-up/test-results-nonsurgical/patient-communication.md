# Follow-up: Test Results + Non-Surgical - Patient Communication

## a. History Taking

### Opening
- Good to see you again. How have you been since our last visit?
- How have things been with your [LOCATION]?

### Interval History
- How has the pain been? Better, worse, or about the same?
- Have you been able to do the exercises / wear the brace / take the medication?
- Any new symptoms or concerns?

### Treatment Adherence
- Have you been able to follow the treatment plan we discussed?
- Any difficulties with the physiotherapy / orthotics?

---

## b. Examination

### Reassessment
- Let me check how things are looking now.
- (Perform focused examination based on condition)

### Compare to Baseline
- The swelling has [improved / stayed the same / increased] since last time.
- The range of motion is [better / unchanged / more limited].
- The tenderness is [less / about the same / more].

### Imaging Review
**Showing and explaining the results:**
- Now let me show you the [X-ray / MRI / CT / Ultrasound] results.

**X-ray findings:**
- The X-ray shows [FINDING IN PLAIN LANGUAGE].
- Here you can see [arthritis / bone spur / alignment / fracture healing].
- The joint space is [narrowed / preserved / normal], which tells us about the cartilage condition.
- The bones are [well-aligned / slightly misaligned / showing signs of wear].

**MRI findings** (if done):
- The MRI gives us detailed soft tissue information.
- This shows [tendon degeneration / partial tear / complete tear / inflammation].
- The cartilage appears [intact / thinned / damaged in this area].
- There's [no / minimal / moderate / significant] fluid/swelling, which indicates inflammation level.

**Ultrasound findings** (if done):
- The ultrasound shows the tendon [thickness / tear / inflammation].
- We can see increased blood flow here, which indicates active inflammation.
- The structure of the [tendon / ligament] is [normal / abnormal / showing degeneration].

**Comparison to clinical findings:**
- These findings match what you're experiencing clinically.
- The imaging confirms that [diagnosis] is the correct diagnosis.

---

## c. Impression

### Results Explanation
- The [X-ray / MRI] shows [FINDING IN PLAIN LANGUAGE].
- This confirms what we suspected: you have [DIAGNOSIS].
- The good news is [POSITIVE FINDING]. However, we can see [RELEVANT PATHOLOGY].

**Common Explanations**:
- "The MRI shows that the tendon has some degeneration but it's not torn."
- "The X-ray shows some arthritis, which explains your symptoms."
- "The scan confirms inflammation but no structural damage, which is good news."

### Progress Assessment
- Overall, you're [improving / staying stable / not improving as much as we'd hoped].
- Your symptoms are [consistent with / better than / worse than] what we'd expect at this stage.

---

## d. Recommendation

### Continue Conservative Management
- Based on these results, I recommend we continue with non-surgical treatment.
- Let's [continue / modify / intensify] the current approach.

### Treatment Modification
- I'd like to add [new treatment modality].
- Let's try [adjustment to current treatment].
- Continue with [effective elements] for another [DURATION].

### Decision Points
- **If you improve over the next [DURATION]**:
  - We'll gradually wean you off support
  - You can start increasing your activities
  - We'll plan for discharge or PRN follow-up

- **If things don't improve or get worse**:
  - We'll consider [injection / surgery / alternative diagnosis]
  - May need additional tests
  - Will reassess the diagnosis

### Next Steps
- I'd like to see you again in [TIMEFRAME] to check your progress.
- In the meantime, continue with [TREATMENT PLAN].
- If anything concerns you before then, please call the clinic.

### Questions & Closing
- Do you have any questions about the results or the plan?
- Any concerns you'd like to discuss?
- Alright, we'll see you in [TIMEFRAME]. Take care.
