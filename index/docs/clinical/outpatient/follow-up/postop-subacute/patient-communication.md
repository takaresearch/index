# Follow-up: Post-Op Subacute - Patient Communication

## a. History Taking

### Opening
- How are you doing? How's the recovery going?
- How far along are you now since the surgery?

### Functional Recovery
- What can you do now that you couldn't do before?
- How far can you walk? Any pain or swelling after activity?
- Are you back to work / normal activities / sports?

### Rehabilitation Progress
- How is the physiotherapy going?
- Have you been able to do the exercises?
- Any difficulties with the rehabilitation program?

### Ongoing Symptoms
- Any pain? If so, when does it happen?
- Any swelling, especially after activity?
- Any stiffness or limitation?

---

## b. Examination

### Reassessment
- Let me see how your range of motion is coming along.
- (Perform functional assessment)

### Findings
- Your range of motion is [much better / improving / limited compared to the other side].
- The swelling is [resolved / minimal / still present but improving].
- Strength is [returning / good / needs more work].
- You're walking [normally / with a slight limp / well considering the timeframe].

### Follow-up Imaging Review
**X-ray assessment:**
- Let me show you today's X-rays and compare them to earlier ones.

**Healing progress:**
- The [bone / osteotomy / fusion site] is healing well / nicely / appropriately.
- We can see [early / good / solid] callus formation, which means new bone is forming.
- The hardware (screws/plate) remains in good position.
- There are no signs of loosening or displacement.

**Visual comparison:**
- **Immediate post-op**: This is right after surgery - you can see the sharp bone edges.
- **Today**: Now you can see the bone is bridging / fusing / healing across the gap.
- The [gap / space] is [closing / filling in with new bone / completely healed].

**For arthrodesis (fusion) cases:**
- The fusion is progressing nicely.
- We can see bone forming across the joint space.
- It's [X%] healed / on track for solid fusion.

**Timeline expectations:**
- At this stage ([X weeks post-op]), we expect to see [early / moderate / advanced] healing.
- Full bone healing typically takes [12-16 weeks / 3-6 months], and you're right on track.

---

## c. Impression

### Recovery Assessment
- Overall, you're doing [very well / well / as expected] for [TIME] after surgery.
- Your function has [significantly improved / improved / is improving gradually].
- The X-rays look good. The [implant / fusion / repair] is [in good position / healing well / progressing as expected].

### Comparative Statement
- Compared to where you were before surgery, you're [much better / improved / on track].
- Most people at this stage are [similar description / comparison to typical recovery].
